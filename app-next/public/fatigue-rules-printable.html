Fatigue Management Rules

WA OSH Regulation 3.132 — Commercial Driver Fatigue

This document lists all rules applied in the Fatigue Record app. Print or save as PDF for reference.

Before starting a shift

  • Minimum 7 hours non-work since last shift. The app blocks “Start shift” until at least 7 hours have passed since the last “End shift” (rolling time; no day boundary).
  • Today’s card must be completed: Rego, Destination, and Start KM must be entered (or carried over) before work time is recorded.

5-hour work / break rule (all drivers)

  • 20 minutes break for every 5 hours work time — at least 10 minutes in one block (e.g. 2×10 min or 1×20 min).
  • Breaks under 10 minutes are counted as work time.
  • Only one violation is raised per “block”; the block resets when a valid break is taken or on “End shift”.

Solo driver rules

Applied when driver type is Solo. Rolling checks use the last 3 days of the previous week when available.

  • ≥7 continuous hours non-work required (within a day that has work).
  • 17-hour rule: Two periods of non-work time (each longer than 7 hours) cannot be separated by more than 17 hours of work and break combined. A 24-hour continuous non-work period resets this rule.
  • ≥27 hours non-work in any rolling 72 hours, including at least 3 blocks of ≥7 continuous hours non-work. A 24-hour continuous non-work period resets this rule.
  • Max 168 hours work in any 14-day period (rolling). The 14-day period resets after ≥48 hours continuous non-work.

Two-Up driver rules

Applied when driver type is Two-Up.

  • ≥7 hours non-work in any rolling 24 hours (can be in a moving vehicle).
  • ≥1 block of ≥7 continuous hours non-work in any rolling 48 hours (not in a moving vehicle).
  • ≥48 hours non-work per 7 days, including at least one block of ≥24 continuous hours non-work.
  • Max 168 hours work in any 14-day period (rolling). Resets after ≥48 hours continuous non-work.

How rules are evaluated

  • Time is recorded as work, break, or non-work in 30-minute slots per calendar day.
  • Rolling rules (17h, 72h, 24h, 48h, 14-day) run over a continuous timeline; the app includes the previous week’s last 3 days where relevant so boundaries between weeks are respected.
  • Declared “24-hour break” (last 24h break date) can reset Solo rolling rules for that boundary.
  • Violations and warnings are shown in the Compliance Check panel and may block or warn before logging work.
